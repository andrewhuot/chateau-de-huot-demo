The Restaurant

A glass-walled room above the lavender fields

La Vérrière occupies the original winter garden of the château — a light-filled room of steel and glass cantilevered over the estate's lower terraces, with panoramic views across the Luberon valley and its sea of lavender below.

Chef Renard's menus change entirely with the seasons, built around a core philosophy of ingredient-first cooking: what grows nearby, what swims nearby, what the land offers at its most abundant. The kitchen maintains direct relationships with seventeen Provençal producers.

Our wine cellar holds 4,200 bottles across 280 references, with a particular depth in the wines of the southern Rhône valley. Sommelier Isabeau Clerc will guide every table through a pairing that illuminates rather than overwhelms.

Executive Chef

Émile Renard

Executive Chef · La Vérrière

Trained under Alain Ducasse at Le Louis XV in Monaco and later at El Bulli during its final extraordinary years, Émile Renard spent two decades absorbing the world's most rigorous culinary traditions before returning to his native Provence. He opened La Vérrière in 2019 with the conviction that the south of France needed no embellishment — only closer attention.

His philosophy is ingredient-first, technique-invisible. Where other chefs reach for complexity, Renard reaches for clarity. A single burrata from a farm eight kilometres away, a fig picked at its moment of perfect ripeness — these are the foundations of his cooking. The two Michelin stars arrived not as a surprise, but as a recognition of something that had always been obvious to anyone who ate here.

"The land tells me what to cook. I simply listen." — Chef Émile Renard
